In this case, we can see that the x-coordinates of the p-adic points seem to come from a global point, alpha, which is a root of x^3 + 3x^2 +2 = f. Then (alpha, alpha^2+2alpha) is a point on the picard curve y^3 = x^4 + x^3 - 3*x^2 - 2*x over the field Q(alpha). In other words (alpha^2 + 2 alpha)^3 is congruent to x^4 + x^3 - 3*x^2 - 2*x mod f. Note though that Q(alpha) has a prime of degree 1 above 7, 11, 13 but not 17 -- if you try at 17, then there are no extra points!
